<div class="pub-entry">
  <h3>
    <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11111941" target="_blank">
      The Impact of RFF and Ensemble Modeling Approach in an E-commerce Product Ranking
    </a>
  </h3>
  <div class="pub-meta">
    <span class="venue-badge">IEEE</span>
    <span class="pub-year">2024</span>
  </div>
  <p>
    This paper investigates how Recency-Frequency-Financial (RFF) signals — adapted from classical customer value analysis — can be incorporated into ensemble ranking models to improve product ordering quality in e-commerce search. By blending behavioural purchase signals with traditional relevance features, the approach achieves measurable improvements in ranking metrics, demonstrating that purchase-pattern features carry significant predictive power beyond click and impression data alone.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="pub-entry">
  <h3>
    <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15724" target="_blank">
      Instruction-Based Fine-Tuning of Open-Source LLMs for Predicting Customer Purchase Behaviors
    </a>
  </h3>
  <div class="pub-meta">
    <span class="venue-badge">arXiv</span>
    <span class="pub-year">2025 · M.Sc. Thesis, Sabancı University</span>
  </div>
  <p>
    This work demonstrates how instruction tuning of open-source large language models can encode customer behavioural signals — browsing sequences, query history, and purchase patterns — to predict downstream purchasing intent. The study compares multiple fine-tuning strategies and shows that lightweight PEFT methods achieve competitive performance against commercial baselines, making the approach practical for real-world deployment in resource-constrained settings.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="pub-entry">
  <h3>
    <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10224005" target="_blank">
      LDA Topic Modeling on Twitter Data Concerning Immigrants and Refugees
    </a>
  </h3>
  <div class="pub-meta">
    <span class="venue-badge">IEEE</span>
    <span class="pub-year">2023</span>
  </div>
  <p>
    Applies Latent Dirichlet Allocation to a large corpus of Turkish-language tweets to surface dominant discourse themes around immigration and refugee topics. The analysis reveals distinct narrative clusters — ranging from policy debates to humanitarian framing — and illustrates how computational text methods can provide structured insight into public sentiment on socially sensitive issues at scale.
  </p>
</div>
