I'm a Senior Data Scientist and ML Engineer at **OLX**, where I build and ship search systems, ranking models, and LLM-powered features for one of the world's largest classifieds platforms. My work sits at the intersection of information retrieval, applied machine learning, and product impact — turning messy, large-scale data into search experiences that users actually notice.

### The Path Here

My career arc runs from early-stage startup to high-scale e-commerce. At **Upily**, I built data infrastructure from scratch — automated ETL pipelines processing millions of daily events, end-to-end churn prediction models, and an LLM assistant that cut API response times by 30%. I also improved user segmentation accuracy by 22%, giving the product team the targeting precision it needed to grow.

From there I joined the Search Algorithm Team at **Hepsiburada**, Turkey's leading e-commerce marketplace. The problems got harder and the scale got bigger. I developed N-gram and topic extraction-based search suggestions that drove a 10% lift in click-through rates and a 7% rise in order conversions. I ran point-wise ranking experiments that yielded an 11% gain in offline metrics, and designed a "Related Search Term" feature powered by BERT and collaborative filtering. These weren't incremental improvements — each one moved real business numbers.

### Research

My academic work sharpened the technical foundation behind the applied work. As a Research Assistant at the **Behavioral Analytics and Visualization Lab**, I worked on a TUBITAK-supported project applying BERT models to Turkish sentiment detection, and fine-tuned large language models for behaviour classification and summarisation tasks. My M.Sc. thesis at **Sabancı University** extended this into a full study on instruction-based fine-tuning of open-source LLMs to predict customer purchase behaviour — work that became [a published paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15724).

### A Different Angle

I started at **Boğaziçi University** studying International Relations and Sociology — graduating first in my cohort with High Honors. That background isn't a detour; it's a differentiator. Understanding how people think, how discourse forms, and how incentives shape behaviour makes me a better ML practitioner. Whether it's designing a ranking system or analysing search intent, the human dimension is never far from the technical one.
